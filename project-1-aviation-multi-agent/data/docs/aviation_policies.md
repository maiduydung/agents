# Aviation Operations Policies & Procedures

## 1. Revenue Management Policies

### 1.1 Dynamic Pricing Strategy
All fare classes must follow the tiered pricing model. Base fares are set at the beginning of each season and adjusted dynamically based on demand signals. The revenue management system monitors booking velocity, competitive fares, and historical load factors to adjust prices within approved bands.

- **Economy Class**: Base fare adjustments allowed within +/- 40% of the seasonal base fare.
- **Premium Economy**: Base fare adjustments allowed within +/- 30%.
- **Business Class**: Base fare adjustments allowed within +/- 25%.

Price increases above 20% from the published fare require Revenue Director approval.

### 1.2 Overbooking Policy
Overbooking is permitted on routes with historical no-show rates above 3%. The maximum overbooking percentage is calculated as:

- **Domestic routes**: Up to 8% overbooking allowed
- **International short-haul (under 6 hours)**: Up to 5% overbooking allowed
- **International long-haul (6+ hours)**: Up to 3% overbooking allowed

When denied boarding occurs, compensation must follow CAAV regulations:
- Flights under 1,000 km: 200 USD equivalent
- Flights 1,000-3,000 km: 400 USD equivalent
- Flights over 3,000 km: 600 USD equivalent

### 1.3 Load Factor Targets
Monthly load factor targets by route type:
- **Trunk routes** (SGN-HAN, HAN-DAD): Target 88-95%
- **Regional routes** (to CXR, PQC, UIH): Target 80-90%
- **International routes**: Target 85-93%

Routes consistently below 75% load factor for 3 consecutive months must undergo route review. Routes below 60% load factor trigger immediate yield management intervention.

### 1.4 Ancillary Revenue
Ancillary revenue targets are set at 15-20% of total ticket revenue. Key ancillary streams include:
- Seat selection fees
- Baggage fees (checked bags beyond allowance)
- In-flight meal pre-orders
- Priority boarding
- Travel insurance

## 2. Flight Operations Policies

### 2.1 Delay Management
Delay categories and required actions:
- **0-15 minutes**: No passenger notification required. Operational log entry only.
- **15-30 minutes**: Gate announcement required. Update departure boards.
- **30-60 minutes**: Passenger notification via SMS/app. Provide complimentary water.
- **60-120 minutes**: Provide meal vouchers. Offer rebooking options.
- **120+ minutes**: Full rebooking assistance. Hotel accommodation for overnight delays.

### 2.2 Gate Assignment Priority
Gate assignments follow this priority order:
1. International departures (immigration processing time)
2. Wide-body aircraft operations
3. Flights with connecting passengers exceeding 30% of total
4. Flights operating to/from weather-affected destinations
5. Standard domestic operations

### 2.3 Turnaround Time Standards
Minimum turnaround times by aircraft type:
- **A320/A321**: 35 minutes (domestic), 50 minutes (international)
- **A350**: 60 minutes (domestic), 90 minutes (international)
- **B787**: 60 minutes (domestic), 90 minutes (international)

### 2.4 Fuel Policy
Tankering fuel is approved when the price differential exceeds 5% between stations. Captains may request additional contingency fuel up to 30 minutes beyond the calculated reserves, subject to performance limitations.

## 3. Crew Management Policies

### 3.1 Flight Time Limitations
Per CAAV regulations aligned with ICAO standards:
- **Maximum flight duty period**: 13 hours (single crew), 16 hours (augmented crew)
- **Maximum flight time**: 9 hours per duty period (single crew)
- **Maximum cumulative flight time**: 100 hours in 28 consecutive days
- **Maximum cumulative flight time**: 1,000 hours in 12 consecutive months
- **Maximum cumulative duty time**: 190 hours in 28 consecutive days

### 3.2 Rest Requirements
Minimum rest periods:
- **Between duty periods**: 10 hours minimum, with opportunity for 8 hours uninterrupted sleep
- **After maximum duty period**: 12 hours minimum rest
- **Weekly rest**: 36 consecutive hours within every 7-day period, including two local nights
- **Extended rest**: One 48-hour rest period within every 14-day period

Crew members who report fatigue may invoke the Fatigue Risk Management policy and must not be penalized.

### 3.3 Crew Assignment Priority
When assigning crew to flights:
1. Check aircraft type rating validity
2. Verify rest compliance (minimum hours met)
3. Check cumulative hours against monthly/annual limits
4. Consider base location to minimize deadhead positioning
5. Balance flight hours across crew members for equitable scheduling
6. Senior crew prioritized for international and long-haul operations

### 3.4 Fatigue Risk Management
Fatigue risk scores are calculated using the Bio-Mathematical Fatigue Model:
- **Low risk (0.0-0.3)**: Normal operations permitted
- **Moderate risk (0.3-0.6)**: Enhanced monitoring, crew briefing required
- **High risk (0.6-0.8)**: Additional crew member recommended, captain discretion
- **Critical risk (0.8-1.0)**: Flight crew must be replaced, no exceptions

### 3.5 Training Requirements
All flight crew must complete:
- Recurrent simulator training every 6 months
- Line check every 12 months
- CRM (Crew Resource Management) training annually
- Emergency procedures review every 12 months
- Security awareness training annually

## 4. Ground Operations

### 4.1 Baggage Handling
Maximum connection times for baggage transfer:
- **Domestic to domestic**: 45 minutes
- **Domestic to international**: 60 minutes
- **International to domestic**: 90 minutes (customs clearance)
- **International to international**: 60 minutes

### 4.2 Passenger Services
Priority boarding order:
1. Passengers requiring assistance (wheelchair, unaccompanied minors)
2. Business class passengers
3. Premium economy passengers
4. Frequent flyer program elite members
5. Economy passengers (back-to-front boarding)

## 5. Safety & Compliance

### 5.1 Reporting Requirements
All safety events must be reported within:
- **Accidents**: Immediately to CAAV and airline safety department
- **Serious incidents**: Within 2 hours
- **Incidents**: Within 24 hours
- **Hazard reports**: Within 72 hours

### 5.2 Weather Minimums
Operations below published approach minimums are not permitted. Special airport qualifications are required for:
- Airports with non-precision approaches only
- Airports with terrain challenges (e.g., mountainous approaches)
- Airports with restricted operating hours
